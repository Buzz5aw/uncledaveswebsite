About Us

ABOUT OUR COMPANY

New Jersey Clean Energy Solutions LLC is a premier company in providing solar energy installation for homeowners and small businesses. By extending our professional expertise throughout the process, which begins in our consulting services and extends past our solar system installations, we have gained the reputation that allows our company to grow throughout New Jersey. NJ Clean Energy Solutions is committed to providing a one on one unique customer experience for every one of our clients. We focus on maximizing your solar energy system production, which increases your return on investment (ROI.) This is accomplished by optimizing solar panel tilt, azimuth and any shading factors. NJ Clean Energy Solutions guarantees the best-designed system with the highest kWh/yearly production output possible. NJ Clean Energy Solutions prides itself on staying updated on the industry's latest installation practices. All of the company's installers have undergone professional training and have been certified by the NABCEP (North American Board of Certified Energy Practitioners).

The Owners

New Jersey Clean Energy Solutions is Located in central New Jersey and is owned by David Widi Sr. and Bryan Hagan.

Bryan has over 20 years of experience relating to design engineering and construction. His passion for green sustainable energy has fueled a desire to keep him on the cutting edge of technologies and innovations. Bryan holds various OSHA certifications relating to construction. He also holds a "LEED GA" certification, this credential recognizes Bryan as a leader in energy and environmental design. He is also recognized by "NABCEP" as a member of the North American Board of Certified Energy Practitioners

David has over 20 years construction experience. Since 2004 He built sustainable energy efficient homes and converted old energy hungry homes to green. His goal was to build near net zero homes by using solar panels, geothermal heating and cooling, solar or tank less water heaters, energy efficient doors, windows, appliances, and lights. Most important the homes were sealed with spray foam insulation to conserve the power we do use.
